Depositional Mechanism of Viscoplastic Mudflows

In this work, scaling of rheological parameters are proposed in a dimensional analysis. It is successfully used in numerical simulations to bridge the depositional behaviors of mudflows at different scales. The numerical modeling with Bingham and Herschel-Bulkley models are validated against small-scale flume experiments

As a research assistant, I conduct numerical simulation on mudflow depositional mechanism with the open source CFD software OpenFOAM , and use Matlab for post-processing and data analysis. I work on the High Performance Computing(HPC) linux cluster to speed up computation.

In the animation, the volume fraction data from OpenFOAM is used to reconstruct the free surface of the flow.

Depositional Mechanism of granular flows

This work in progress aims at a comprehensive numerical investigation of the depositional mechanism of a finite volume of bi-dispersal granular flow. The effect of various geological and geometrical parameters are considered, including flume geometry, particle size ratio, basal roughness etc.

Numerical simulations are performed with the Open Source Discrete Element Method Particle Simulation Software LIGGGHTS(LAMMPS Improved for General Granular and Granular Heat Transfer Simulations) on a HPC platform.

In the simulation, the sample is comprosed of equal mass of large particles of 10mm diameter and small particles of 5mm diameter, in total 7557 particles. The base of the container is constructed as closely packed small particles. Rapid segregation occurs on the channel, and the particles deposit on the container.

Image processing of flume test

The experimental mudflow flume test was recorded and analyzed with Matlab Optical Flow to extract the profile of the flow. The velocity of the flow can be calculated. The numerical simulation was compared with and validated against profile and the velocity of the flow.
